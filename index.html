DISH vs. DIRECTV:
Who's Got the Best DVR?

We've decided to compare the most popular DVRs Dish and DirecTV have to offer, and we're ready to give our recommendation.

In our research, we wanted to focus on the most important features: recording capacity, internal storage, and simultaneous recording . And because some DVRs rely on specific service plans, we'll provide some insight into what the two companies offer with their DVRs (there's the price too, but we'll get to that).

DIRECTV DVRs

There are currently only four DVRs available directly from DirecTV, and one is not like the others. DirecTV's top model, the Genie (HR44), is packed with features; in fact, when comparing it to other DirecTV DVRs, the Genie is easily the best.

The Genie has a recording capacity of 200 hours in HD (800 hours in SD), a 1 TB internal hard drive, and the capability of recording five channels at once—more than enough for us. The next two options, the HD DVR (HR24) and the TiVo HD DVR (THR22), are essentially the same, but each can only do half of what the Genie does (see table below). As for the standard DVR (R22), its 125 GB hard drive and lack of simultaneous recording makes it the barebones option.

DISH DVRs

We found five DVRs available directly from Dish, and much like DirecTV, Dish's top model, the Hopper (ES197107), stands out from the rest.

The Hopper packs a 2 TB internal hard drive (double DirecTV's Genie), which by our numbers, allows 400 hours of HD recording (1600 hours in SD). It also can record up to eight channels at once (the Genie only does five, but we're still not sure we need more than that.) Compared to the Hopper, other Dish DVRs seem... well, inadequate. The DuoDVR ViP (722k) has a 500 GB internal hard drive (one-fourth of the Hopper), and can only record two shows at once. Subsequent DVRs continue to drop in recording capacity and storage size (see table below).

Wait, What about the Price?

We spoke with representatives from Dish and DirecTV. Currently, both the Genie and the Hopper are "free" for new customers. Of course, "free" means strings attached. Expect a two-year contract and penalties from either company if you end your contract early. There is also an additional monthly fee for the Hopper and the Genie. And your other monthly fees will differ depending on your channel selection and if you've decided to include other packages (e.g., internet service package).

Conclusion

The Hopper earns our recommendation for best DVR.

Though we're skeptical we'll need to record more than five channels at once, the Hopper gives us the kind of recording capacity so we never have to delete our favorite shows, even long after they're over (looking at you Justified.)
